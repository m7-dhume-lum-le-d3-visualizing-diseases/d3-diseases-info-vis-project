Introduction

To better understand the covid-19 situation, we focused on creating a tool for visualizing diseases using an interactive geospatial visualization. We believe having such tools will help people better understand the spread of diseases and our history with combating diseases. According to researcher Diansheng Guo, effective visualization tools using spatial interactions will help decision makers make better preparations in the event of a pandemic [1]. This will also allow users to get a visual idea of how much a disease is spreading, where it is spreading, and to what magnitude it is spreading.
